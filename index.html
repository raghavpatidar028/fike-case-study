Section 03 — My Approach

How I Operate as a Business Systems Analyst

Over 3.5 years at Infosys, I refined a structured approach to BSA work that consistently delivered 95% stakeholder satisfaction and measurable improvements. Here's the methodology I'd bring to Fike from day one.

Step 01

Structured Requirements Elicitation

Requirements workshops must be designed, not improvised. My approach:

  • Pre-workshop agenda and stakeholder prep materials
  • Business context first, solution last — no anchoring to existing tech
  • RACI-mapped SME identification before each session
  • Requirement classification: functional, non-functional, data, integration, compliance
  • Sign-off gate before moving to technical design
Step 02

Process Mapping with Discipline

Current-state documentation reveals what's broken. Future-state documentation aligns the team before development starts.

  • BPMN-compliant swimlane diagrams in Lucidchart
  • System touchpoints and data handoffs explicitly labeled
  • Identify decision points, exception flows, and manual workarounds
  • Version-controlled in Confluence linked to JIRA epics
  • Reviewed and signed off by process owners, not just IT
Step 03

Salesforce Stewardship

Salesforce org health is not a one-time cleanup — it's an ongoing discipline requiring proactive ownership.

  • Monthly org health check: unused flows, permission sprawl, stale records
  • Seasonal release sandbox preview 4–6 weeks before go-live
  • Feature impact assessment with business stakeholders before adoption
  • CPQ and Field Service rule reviews aligned to sales process changes
  • Rootstock ERP integration monitoring for data consistency
Step 04

Agile Delivery in Practice

Agile is a mindset, not just a meeting cadence. My Scrum habits from Infosys:

  • User stories: role → goal → value, with explicit acceptance criteria
  • Sprint planning driven by business priority, not tech preference
  • Backlog groomed weekly — no stale items, no ambiguous epics
  • Retrospective actions tracked and closed in next sprint
  • JIRA boards maintained in real time, not updated at standups
Step 05

QA and UAT Leadership

Quality is built in, not bolted on. My testing approach:

  • Test cases written from acceptance criteria, not developer assumptions
  • Business stakeholders co-own UAT — not just IT signing off
  • Regression suite maintained and expanded with every release
  • Defect triage and severity classification within 24 hours of discovery
  • Go/no-go decision framework documented before every deployment
Step 06

Stakeholder Communication

Transparency is a risk management tool. Senior leadership can only make good decisions with accurate status information.

  • Weekly status emails: RAG status, milestones, blockers, decisions needed
  • Power BI dashboard for project portfolio visibility
  • Risk log updated biweekly with mitigation plans
  • Escalation protocol defined before a project starts, not during a crisis
  • Post-launch hypercare report at Day 7, Day 14, Day 30
