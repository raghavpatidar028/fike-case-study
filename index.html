03 — My Approach

How I Work as a BSA

Refined across 3.5 years at Infosys. Not theory — habits I practice on every project.

01

Requirements Elicitation

  • Pre-built workshop agendas sent in advance
  • Business context before solutions — no tech anchoring
  • RACI-mapped SMEs identified before each session
  • Sign-off gate before technical design begins
02

Process Mapping

  • BPMN swimlane diagrams in Lucidchart
  • System touchpoints and data handoffs labeled
  • Exception flows and manual workarounds captured
  • Versioned in Confluence, linked to JIRA epics
03

Salesforce Stewardship

  • Monthly org health: flows, permissions, stale records
  • Sandbox preview 4–6 weeks before each seasonal release
  • CPQ + Field Service rules reviewed with business alignment
  • Rootstock ERP integration monitoring for data consistency
04

Agile Delivery

  • User stories: role → goal → value with acceptance criteria
  • Sprint planning driven by business priority, not tech preference
  • Backlog groomed weekly — no stale or ambiguous epics
  • Retro actions tracked and closed in the next sprint
05

QA & UAT Leadership

  • Test cases written from acceptance criteria, not dev assumptions
  • Business stakeholders co-own UAT — not just IT sign-off
  • Regression suite maintained and expanded per release
  • Go/no-go framework documented before every deploy
06

Stakeholder Comms

  • Weekly RAG status: milestones, blockers, decisions needed
  • Power BI project health dashboard for leadership
  • Escalation protocol defined before a crisis, not during one
  • Day 7 / 14 / 30 hypercare reports after every go-live
